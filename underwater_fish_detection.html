Underwater Fish Detection and Tracking System

AI-based vision system for underwater vehicles

Project Overview

While working at Cyprus Robotics in collaboration with Orca - Caretta Robotics, I helped to develop an AI-based vision system for autonomous underwater vehicles. This system was designed to detect, classify, and track various fish species in real-time, even in challenging underwater environments with limited visibility.

The development of this system involved implementing deep learning models based on CNN architecture specifically optimized for underwater environments. The system overcomes common challenges in underwater vision such as light scattering, color distortion, and particulate matter, providing reliable detection in various marine conditions.

System Demonstration

The fish detection and species classification algorithms were designed to be computationally efficient, allowing them to run on the embedded systems of autonomous underwater vehicles with limited processing power. The system achieved over 85% accuracy in species identification even in turbid water conditions and could track multiple targets simultaneously with minimal latency.

Technical Challenges

The development of this underwater vision system presented several unique technical challenges:

  • Compensating for underwater light scattering and absorption that affects image quality
  • Developing robust algorithms that could handle varying visibility conditions
  • Creating efficient models that could run on power-constrained underwater platforms
  • Building a dataset of underwater fish images for training the detection models
  • Implementing real-time tracking capabilities that could handle the dynamic underwater environment

The solution incorporated advanced preprocessing techniques to enhance underwater imagery, transfer learning to minimize the required training data, and custom model optimization to ensure performance on embedded hardware platforms.

Applications

This fish detection and tracking system has been implemented in several applications:

  • Marine life monitoring for environmental research
  • Autonomous underwater surveillance systems
  • Fishery management and stock assessment
  • Coral reef ecosystem monitoring
  • Debris detection and environmental cleanup operations

The system provides valuable data for marine biologists and environmental scientists while enhancing the capabilities of autonomous underwater vehicles for both research and commercial applications.
